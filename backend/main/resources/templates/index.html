Hindu Heritage Hall

Preserving and Celebrating Hindu Culture and Heritage

Overview

The Hindu Heritage Hall (HHH) is an audio-visual interactive exhibit hall about Hindu heritage and culture. It is a learning center and art gallery for people of all ages, especially the next generation, curious about their roots.

Located in the Administration Building at the Shiva-Vishnu Temple in Livermore, the HHH is the first-of-its-kind initiative created by volunteers for the community.

Hours: Open on Saturday and Sunday, 10:00 AM to 6:00 PM. Admission: Free.

Exhibits

The exhibits cover various topics such as Bharat's history and geography, Hindu religion and culture, art and crafts, literature, and entertainment. These include:

  • Ravi Varma Paintings

  • Stained Glass Art: "Rama Pattabhishekam" and "Gita Upadesham"
  • Bharat Timeline Wall Chart and Workstation
  • Jeopardy Video Wall with trivia games
  • Selfie Stations for photos at Rama's and Krishna's feet
  • Library of Hindu Heritage books

These exhibits are only available in person at the Heritage Hall.

Volunteer Opportunities

The Hindu Heritage Hall is an all-volunteer initiative. We welcome students, youth, and seniors to help in the following areas:

  • Monitoring Duty: Keeping the hall open on weekends
  • IT Support: Ensuring exhibits run smoothly
  • Content Creation: Crafting and curating exhibit content

We especially need individuals knowledgeable about Bharat's history and culture, and skilled in graphic arts and design.

Curator and Special Committee

The curator of the Hindu Heritage Hall is Vijay Sampath, who also serves as the Chairman of the HCCC Special Committee. This committee is responsible for planning, budgeting, and community engagement.
